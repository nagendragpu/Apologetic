Premise 1: Everything that begins to exist has a cause

This is virtually undeniable for any sincere truth-seeker! If something came into being without a cause, it would have to come into being out of nothing. Here are some supporting reasons to uphold the veracity of this premise:

1. Something cannot come from nothing!

This is based on the simple and well-known law of causality- that every effect has a cause. The famous Latin phrase explains it futher: Ex nihilo nihil fit- Out of nothing, nothing comes. This is not astrophysics or rocket science, this is common sense!


Pop Science theories may have stipulated that some virtual particles come out of nothing but their problem is the incorrect definition of 'nothing'. 'Nothing' is not just a vaccum- absence of matter, but rather 'nothing' is the absence of space itself! It would thereby have no properties whatsoever - including that of extremely-ordered-universe-spawning-properties as well. To belive this would be a leap of faith, indeed this would be irrational faith which is founded contrary to the evidence!

2. 'Nothing' would end up being discriminatory.

If it is possible for something to come out of 'nothing', then why is it that only universes are spawned from 'nothing'? It would be equally likely for Mozart or a Quarter pounder McDonald's burger to spawn out of nothing as well! This assertion seems to make 'nothingness' have attributes such as favouring the spawning of universes and this is absurd because 'nothingness' has no attributes!

3. Scientific evidence and our day-to-day experience confirm this truth.

This premise has always been verified by our common experience and never falsified. There are no good reasons to hold to the antithesis!

Premise 2: The Universe began to exist

The Aristotelian view of an eternal universe has been around for very long, alongside which the Jewish view of a created cosmos has also been around for quite some time. Is the Universe eternal or did the Universe have an absolute beginning? Here are some good reasons to believe that the Universe had a beginning:

Philosophical Argument

If the Universe never had a beginning, then we would have to cross an actual infite number of days in the past to reach the present moment/current day. But stipulating that it is possible to come to the present day in a beginningless universe aka 'to cross an actual infinite' is akin to saying it is plausible for Bob to jump out of an infinitely-deep pit!


This is not rational at all, just as when you hear Lisa saying she finished counting down all the negative-numbers today as she finally groaned "Zero..".
It is not possible to count up to infinity (0,1,2...) and similarily is not possible to count down from infinity (...-2,-1,0).
What would you conclude when you scroll to this section of this article and someone tells you that this article had no beginning? You would conclude that it definitely had one since you have reached this present section!


Hence, since we have reached this present moment, and we know that an infinite regress is impossible i.e it is impossible to cross an actual infinity; reason demands that we conclude that the Universe had a beginning.

Scientific Argument

According to the second law of thermodynamics, unless energy is being fed into a system, that system will become increasingly disorderly. Which means that given enough time, the Universe will run out of useable energy and eventually reach a point of 'heat death' where there will be 'no light, no heat, and no motion'.


If the Universe will, in a finite time, reach equilibrium aka 'heat death', and if the Universe is eternal in the past i.e it has no beginning, then it should have reached this state of heat death already. But we are not in this state, hence we know that the Universe had a finite past aka the universe had a beginning.


It is said that an argument is what convinces reasonable men and a proof is what it takes to convince even an unreasonable man. With the proof now in place, cosmologists can no longer hide begin the possibility of a past-eternal universe. There is no escape: the have to face the problem of a cosmic beginning.

-Alexander Vilenkin

Conclusion: The Universe has a cause

This cause of the Universe must transcend the Universe to be able to create it. Let us probe into some of the attributes this cause wuold possess:

Uncaused: Since we have seen that an infinite regress is impossible, the cause of the Universe would have to be the 'Uncased First Cause'. Or as Aristotle said 'The Unmoved Mover'.

Timeless, Immaterial, and Non-physical: The cause of the Universe would have to transcend space and time since it created space and time. Hence the cause would be outside of time aka timeless, and outside of space aka immaterial and non-physical.

Unimaginably Powerful: The cause of the Universe would be responsible for creating all matter and energy and hence would be unimaginably powerful.

Personal: The only way to have an infinite cause (here, the Uncaused First Cause) and a finite effect (here, the Universe) would be if the cause had the freedom of the will to create.

In Conclusion, The Cosmological Argument gives us good reason to believe in an Uncaused, Timeless, Spaceless, Immaterial, Non-physical, and Unimaginably Powerful Cause of the Universe much like whom theists define as 'God'.
