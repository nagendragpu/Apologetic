Premise 1: If God does not exist, objective moral values and duties do not exist.

Let's get some key definitions clarified:

Moral Values: Values have to do with what is good and bad i.e something's worth. For example, becoming a nurse is a good thing.

Moral Duties: Duties have to do with that which obligatory and binding upon us.
The Moral Values are not obligatory just as becoming a nurse is good, but not binding upon us.

Objective: That which is independent of people's opinion. Example: Hitler killing the Jews is objectively wrong irrespective of his beliefs.

Subjective: That which is dependent upon people's opinions. Example: Bob thinks Vanilla ice-cream tastes better than Chocolate.

Let's think this through:

If there is no God, then we do not have any objective reference point so as to differentiate between good and evil. God's nature provides this objective reference point for moral values, since it serves as the standard of measure of morality (just as a scale measures length). To put it more crudely, if our existence is merely a chance collocation of atoms on a bed of dust and we are just bags of protoplasm floating in space, then all we are left with is one person's viewpoint which is subjective and non-binding upon all rather than objective. Each individual could have their own personal preferences of what is moral to them and they all could be different (just as our subjective choice of ice-cream flavours).

The universe that we observe has precisely the properties we should expect if there is, at bottom, no design, no purpose, no evil, no good, nothing but pitiless indifference.

-Richard Dawkins (Atheist), River out of Eden: A Darwinian view of Life

Hence if God does not exists, all we are left with is sujective moral values which are non-obligatory and non-binding due to the lack of an objective reference point.

Premise 2: Objective moral values and duties do exist.

The Holocaust

Was it wrong for Hitler to kill 6 million Jews?

Is ethical cleansing always wrong?

Does it matter whether he thought he was doing the 'right' thing?

Torturing babies for fun

Is it wrong to torture babies for fun?

Is it always wrong?

Is it wrong even if someone else thinks it's a good thing?

Rape

Is it wrong for Person A to rape a fellow human being?

Is rape always wrong?

Is it wrong even if Person A thinks it's a good thing?

Clearly there are certain acts that we realize are 'good' or 'bad' regardless of people's opinions about them.

Conclusion: Therefore, God exists.

Note: 1. This argument does not stipulate that an atheist cannot lead a 'morally good' life. Rather, this argument shows how inept atheism is of quaifying terms such as 'good' and 'bad' since if there is no objective reference point, it is merely subjective.

2. This argument does not with Moral Epistemology- that God is the source of finding out what is 'good' and 'bad', rather it deals with Moral Ontology- that the very nature of 'good' and 'bad is grounded in God's nature.

In Conclusion, The Moral Argument gives us good reason to believe that God exists and He is perfectly good, and that his nature is the objective reference point with respect to Moral values.
